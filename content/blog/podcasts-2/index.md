It’s just about time to retire a notebook. The [Moleskine Classic Notebook Expanded](https://www.moleskine.com/en-us/shop/notebooks/the-legendary-notebook/classic-notebook-expanded-black-8058647628028.html) is the regular notebook, but it has about 50% more pages. I started using this one in 2019. Flipping the pages is a welcome reflection on personal progress and growth.
1. For a couple of books now I have made a list of incomplete goals on the inside cover. Looks like I actually checked some things off including writing a blog post called [Remember New Jack City](/blog/new-jack-city), transitioning my job to DesignOps, creating a [digital bookshelf](/blog/bookshelf/). Some things I have still not done is writing a blog post called *The Cosmic Perspective* (I have forgotten what this means), Standup Paddleboarding, doing more illustration, and some other things. 
1. One of these books always seems to start and end with notes from a conference. This one starts with one of the last An Event Apart events in Boston. There are 18 pages of notes. Many are still inspiring. I went with my co-worker Henry. I think he would agree that one of the highlights was eating dinner with Brad Frost and Dan Mall.  
1. There are many drafts of messages written in cards. Weddings, birthdays, one post card message about travel to Canada on a hiking trip. There is some record of me going on a hiking trip to the Yukon Territory of Canada. This is a really cool hiking trip, but the note is about writing a postcard to my mom talking about the totem poles in Stanley park in Vancouver. 
1. I worked on a website for a Reki therapist. The nots include 6 personas, some guidance for my friend who did illustrations for the site.
1. Negative and positive bullet points about a candidate interivew. I don’t think we hired that person. There is another page that lists some details about a job that I was interested in. 
1. A sketch of an *Alaska chair*. Instructions are also provided with the sketch. Farmhouse hack: Cut a hole* in the bottom of an Aderondack chair. It becomes and Alaska Chair.
